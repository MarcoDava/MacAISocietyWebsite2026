NEW MacAI Website 
Background and Goals 
The MacAI society’s existing site is primarily a MacHacks landing page. Feedback from stakeholders 
indicates that the current site is static and text‑heavy, lacks emotional appeal, contains weak 
calls‑to‑action and doesn’t reflect a cohesive identity for a broader AI society. The goal of the 
redesign is to create a legitimate and energetic online home for McMaster AI Society (MacAI) and its 
flagship events, projects and community. The new site should be future‑focused, community‑driven 
and credible while remaining approachable. It must also align with the 2026 brand guidelines (colour 
palette, typography, logo use and tone of voice) and incorporate modern web‑design trends. 
• Logo usage – maintain the integrity of the MacAI logo by adhering to prescribed size, spacing 
and colour specifications; do not warp, rotate or alter logo colours. 
• Colour palette – primary colours include #1800AD, #1CB1E3, #3DDFF5, #A7C2C3, 
#F0F4F4 and #E00064. Use these consistently across the site, with darker blues/purples 
(#1800AD) and teals (#1CB1E3/#3DDFF5) for backgrounds and highlights and the pink/red 
(#E00064) sparingly for calls‑to‑action. 


• Typography – approved fonts are Montserrat (bold or regular for headings) and Roboto (book 
or bold for body text). Ensure type sizes create clear hierarchy and maintain readability. 
• Imagery & tone of voice – visuals should support MacAI’s narrative and feel trustworthy, 
down‑to‑earth and informative. Copy should use clear, plain‑language explanations and focus 
on benefits and relevance while avoiding buzzwords, elitism or fear‑based messaging.

High‑Level Aesthetic Direction 
1. Future‑focused yet welcoming: Use the dark purple (#1800AD) and teal (#1CB1E3/#3DDFF5) 
tones as dominant backgrounds with gradients and soft transitions to convey a futuristic feel. 
Integrate subtle micro‑animations – buttons that lift or change colour on hover, cards that 
slightly scale, and stats that count up – to add energy without overwhelming the user. 
2. Custom AI‑themed illustrations: Replace stock photos with bespoke illustrations showing 
students collaborating on AI projects, robotics and data‑visualization. These can incorporate 3D 
or gradient shading to match trending aesthetic[2]. Use photography (photos of events, workshops 
and hackathons) sparingly, focusing on community and diversity – the gallery should showcase 
students smiling, working and presenting. 
3. Full‑page hero sections: Each top‑level page should open with a full‑width section pairing a 
strong headline and brief subheading with a high‑quality illustration or background video. Place 
the headline and CTA on the left and the visual on the right, as users’ attention is drawn to the 
top‑left area[3]. 
4. Purposeful white space and clean navigation: Adopt a minimalist layout with generous 
white/negative space[5]. Use card‑based sections to group information (e.g., events, projects, 
sponsors). Navigation should be simplified: collapse rarely used pages into a “More” menu and 
ensure the primary nav is always visible. 
5. Responsive and accessible: Ensure the design translates seamlessly to mobile; more than half of 
web traffic is mobile, and responsive layouts with readable text and easy‑to‑tap CTAs are 
essential[7][8]. Provide alt text for images, captions for videos and clear forms. 
6. Consistent tone: Write copy in a trustworthy, down‑to‑earth voice. Highlight what MacAI helps 
students do (learn AI, build projects, join community) and avoid hype or fear‑based hooks.
